VLSI Design

VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) involves designing and verifying integrated circuits by combining millions of transistors into a single chip. It plays a crucial role in modern electronics, powering devices from smartphones to supercomputers. My focus in VLSI includes digital design, HDL coding, verification, and design optimization.

Digital Logic & HDL

  • Strong understanding of combinational & sequential logic circuits.
  • Implemented adders, counters, multiplexers, registers in Verilog.
  • Designed finite state machines (FSMs) for real-world applications.
  • Well-versed in RTL coding best practices.

Simulation & Verification

  • Experience with testbench creation and functional verification.
  • Debugged timing issues and logical errors in simulation tools.
  • Applied stimulus-response methods for verifying modules.
  • Knowledge of waveform analysis for debugging circuit behavior.

Design Optimization

  • Analyzed trade-offs between power, performance, and area (PPA).
  • Worked on reducing propagation delay and minimizing power usage.
  • Familiar with synchronous design and clock domain crossing concepts.
  • Exposure to timing closure methods in digital circuits.

Tools & Technologies

  • HDL: Verilog (primary), basics of VHDL.
  • Simulation: ModelSim, Xilinx ISE/ Vivado (exposure).
  • Knowledge of synthesis and basic FPGA implementation flow.
  • Familiarity with EDA tools for functional verification.

Applications & Interest

  • Microprocessor and ASIC design.
  • FPGA-based prototyping and testing.
  • Low-power circuit design for IoT and embedded systems.
  • Exploring opportunities in semiconductor design industry.
