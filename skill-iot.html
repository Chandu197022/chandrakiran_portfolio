Internet of Things (IoT)

IoT is the bridge between physical sensors and cloud intelligence. In practice, building IoT systems requires attention to hardware selection, signal conditioning, and robust firmware. For embedded nodes I commonly use ESP32 or Arduino-class MCUs; they provide Wi-Fi/BLE for connectivity and sufficient I/O for sensors.

Sensor calibration and debouncing are crucial — raw sensor readings are noisy and must be filtered before decisions. Power budgeting matters in field deployments, especially when battery-operated. Edge logic (simple thresholds, state machines) saves bandwidth and reduces cloud costs.

Connectivity protocols matter: MQTT is lightweight and ideal for telemetry, while HTTP/REST works for simple uploads. Security is often overlooked — secure provisioning and credential management should be baked into the firmware. OTA updates keep devices maintainable.
